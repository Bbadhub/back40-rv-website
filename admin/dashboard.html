Getting Your First 10 Reviews

Why 10 reviews? Studies show businesses with 10+ reviews get significantly more clicks and bookings.

Step 1: Identify Past Guests

Make a list of 15-20 past guests who had positive experiences. You want more than 10 in case some don't respond.

Step 2: Personalize Your Request

Don't send generic messages. Personal touches work better:

"Hi John! Hope you and Sarah are doing well. We loved having you stay with us last month and were so glad you enjoyed the Back 40 trails. Would you mind leaving us a quick Google review? It really helps us out. Here's the link: [YOUR LINK]"

Step 3: Make It Easy

  • Include the direct review link (don't make them search)
  • Send via their preferred method (text or email)
  • Timing: Ask within 1-2 weeks of their stay while it's fresh

Step 4: Follow Up (Politely)

If someone doesn't respond after a week, one gentle follow-up is okay: "Hey, just wanted to bump this up in case you missed it!"

Step 5: Respond to Every Review

Thank reviewers and include keywords in your response:

"Thanks so much for staying at our Bella Vista RV park with trail access! We're glad you enjoyed the Back 40 trails and hope to see you again soon."

Step 6: Create On-Site System

  • Print a QR code linking to your review page
  • Post it near checkout area or on your signage
  • Mention it at check-in: "If you enjoy your stay, we'd love a review!"
