Bentonville, Arkansas

World-Class Mountain Biking Capital

Bentonville has transformed into one of the premier mountain biking destinations in the United States. With 137+ miles of trails across 53+ trail systems, this small Arkansas city punches far above its weight class.

But Bentonville isn't just about bikes. Walmart's headquarters here has brought incredible cultural attractions, world-class dining, and a vibrant downtown square that rivals major metropolitan areas.

Top Attractions

  • Crystal Bridges Museum of American Art - Free admission, world-class art collection, stunning architecture by Moshe Safdie
  • The Momentary - Contemporary art space with rotating exhibits, performances, and rooftop bar
  • Walmart Museum - Sam Walton's original 5&10 store on the town square
  • Downtown Bentonville Square - Shops, restaurants, breweries, bike rentals
  • Compton Gardens - Beautiful public gardens, free admission

Dining Highlights

  • The Preacher's Son - Upscale dining in a converted church
  • Blu Fresh Fish Marketplace - Coastal seafood, sushi bar, and fresh fish market
  • Tusk & Trotter - Farm-to-table American cuisine
  • Oven and Tap - Pizza, craft beer, bike-friendly patio
  • Table Mesa Bistro - Latin-inspired cuisine and cocktails
  • Bike Rack Brewing - Local brewery with great food

Crystal Bridges Museum - Free world-class art in Bentonville

Mountain Biking Culture

Bentonville's transformation into a mountain biking destination is nothing short of remarkable. Driven by Walmart heir Tom and Steuart Walton's passion for cycling, the area has seen hundreds of millions in investment into trail systems, bike infrastructure, and cycling culture.

What Makes It Special

  • 250+ miles of trails within 30-minute drive
  • World-class trail design by IMBA-certified builders
  • Year-round riding - trails maintained 12 months
  • Bike-friendly infrastructure - protected lanes, bike parking everywhere
  • Welcoming community - beginner to pro riders

Major Events

  • Bikes Blues & BBQ (Oct 1-4, 2025) - Largest motorcycle rally in the U.S.
  • OZ Trails Off-Road - Annual MTB festival and races
  • Pedaling for Pancakes - Charity ride and breakfast
  • Various NICA races - High school mountain bike leagues

Pro tip: Many Bentonville restaurants and breweries have bike wash stations and welcome muddy riders!

Bella Vista, Arkansas

Trail Access & Outdoor Recreation

Bella Vista is home to the famous Little Sugar Trail System - 50 miles of purpose-built mountain biking singletrack that draws riders from across the country. The trails are maintained year-round and offer something for every skill level.

Little Sugar Trails

  • 50 miles of singletrack
  • Open 5 AM - 10 PM daily
  • Beginner to expert terrain
  • Well-marked with trail maps at trailheads
  • Dog-friendly (leashed)

Services & Amenities

  • Grocery stores (Walmart, Harps)
  • Gas stations and convenience stores
  • Restaurants and cafes
  • Bike shops with rentals and service
  • Medical facilities and urgent care

Worth the Drive - Day Trips

Expand your adventure with these nearby destinations

Table Rock Lake (45 min)

One of the best fishing and boating lakes in the Midwest. Swimming, kayaking, paddleboarding, and lakeside dining. Beautiful scenery and clear water.

Branson, Missouri (60 min)

Famous entertainment destination with live shows, Silver Dollar City theme park, shopping outlets, and family attractions. Known as "Nashville of the Ozarks."

Devil's Den State Park (45 min)

Stunning Arkansas state park with caves, hiking trails, mountain biking, and rock formations. Popular for photography and primitive camping.

Fayetteville, Arkansas (50 min)

College town (University of Arkansas) with vibrant dining scene, Dickson Street entertainment district, Razorback Stadium, and Botanical Garden of the Ozarks.

Pea Ridge National Military Park (30 min)

Historic Civil War battlefield with driving tour, hiking trails, and visitor center. One of the most intact Civil War battlefields in the nation.

Wiederkehr Wine Cellars (60 min)

Arkansas wine country near Altus. Tours, tastings, restaurant, and beautiful vineyard views. Great for a relaxing afternoon.

Local Tips from Mark

Insider knowledge to enhance your visit

Best Times to Visit

Museums: Weekday mornings for smaller crowds

Restaurants: Make reservations for popular spots on weekends

Trails: Early morning or late afternoon for cooler temps

Parking Tips

Bentonville Square: Free parking in lots behind the square

Crystal Bridges: Free parking, arrive early on weekends

Trailheads: Most have ample free parking

Money Savers

Free: Crystal Bridges, Compton Gardens, most trails

Deals: Many restaurants have happy hour specials

Walmart: Best prices for groceries and RV supplies

Stay Connected

WiFi: Available at most coffee shops and restaurants

Trail Apps: Download Trailforks, AllTrails, MTB Project before arrival

Events: Check local Facebook groups for rides and events
